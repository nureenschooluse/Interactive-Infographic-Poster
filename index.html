Sperm Health

The 4 Key Indicators of Male Fertility

Did you know?

According to the World Health Organization, male fertility can be affected by sperm count, movement, shape, and overall sperm quality. Lifestyle factors such as smoking, obesity, and excessive alcohol consumption may negatively impact sperm health.

Click indicators & hover over the anatomy below

1. Sperm Count

What is it?
The number of sperm present in semen.

WHO Benchmark: ≥ 15 million sperm per mL

✅

Higher count increases the likelihood of fertilization.

⚠️

Low count may reduce the chances of conception.

3. Morphology

What is it?
The size and shape of sperm cells.

WHO Benchmark: ≥ 4% normally shaped

✅

Normally shaped sperm are more likely to fertilize an egg.

⚠️

Abnormal structures can affect fertility.

2. Motility

What is it?
The ability of sperm to move efficiently.

WHO Benchmark: ≥ 40% moving overall

✅

Healthy sperm must swim forward to reach the egg.

⚠️

Poor motility makes fertilization more difficult.

4. Semen Volume

What is it?
The amount of semen released.

WHO Benchmark: ≥ 1.5 mL

✅

Adequate volume helps transport sperm safely.

⚠️

Low volume may indicate reproductive issues.

Myth vs. Fact

Myth

"Infertility is usually a woman's problem."

Fact

Male factors contribute to approximately 50% of all infertility cases globally.

Myth

"Heat doesn't affect sperm."

Fact

Testicles need to be cooler than body temp. Hot tubs, saunas, and laptops on laps can severely reduce production.

Myth

"Age only affects female fertility."

Fact

While men produce sperm their whole lives, sperm quality, count, and motility decline significantly after age 40.

How to Maintain Healthy Sperm

Healthy Habits

  • Eat a balanced diet rich in fruits & vegetables
  • Exercise regularly & maintain healthy body weight
  • Get 7–9 hours of sleep & manage stress effectively

Things to Avoid

  • Avoid smoking & limit alcohol intake
  • Avoid anabolic steroids & recreational drugs
  • Reduce prolonged exposure to excessive heat
